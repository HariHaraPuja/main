Jagran Puja

Jagran Puja is a devotional all-night prayer and chanting session, typically dedicated to Lord Shiva, Goddess Durga, or other deities, where devotees stay awake and engage in spiritual practices to seek blessings, remove obstacles, and gain divine favor. It involves singing bhajans, kirtans, and reciting mantras in a group setting, accompanied by the lighting of lamps or diyas. The purpose of Jagran Puja is to invoke the deity's blessings for peace, prosperity, health, and the removal of negativity. It is often performed during festivals, especially Navratri, or when seeking divine intervention during times of distress. The collective energy of devotion and celebration is believed to purify the mind and soul, bringing divine protection and fulfillment of wishes.

Mata Ki Chowki

Mata Ki Chowki is a religious gathering and devotional event dedicated to worshipping Goddess Durga or other deities. It involves the congregation of devotees who sit together to chant bhajans, kirtans, and hymns in praise of the goddess, typically conducted in the evening or night. The puja is often performed at home or in community spaces, where an altar or "chowki" (platform) is set up with the idol or picture of the goddess. The event is usually accompanied by the lighting of diyas (lamps), offering of prasad (sweets, fruits), and singing devotional songs, creating a spiritually uplifting atmosphere. Mata Ki Chowki is believed to bring divine blessings, peace, prosperity, and protection to the devotees. It is commonly organized during festivals like Navratri, or during specific occasions when devotees seek the goddess's grace for personal well-being, success, and the removal of obstacles.
